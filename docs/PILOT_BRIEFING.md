# Welcome to Area Code

Three pages. Read the whole thing. It pays for the time it took to write.

## What this is

Area Code is a check-in app. Customers walk in, tap a button, get points toward a free thing on you. The free thing is yours to design — a coffee, a side, a percent off, whatever makes sense at your venue.

There's no card. No discount table. No barcode. No app on your till.

The whole platform runs on a phone in your customer's pocket and a phone in your staff member's pocket.

## What you're agreeing to do

We give you the platform free for 14 days. After that, R299 a month if you stay, no contract. Cancel any time.

In return, we ask three things during the trial:

1. **Set up one reward.** A free coffee at the 5th visit is the simplest. Takes 2 minutes in the dashboard.
2. **Brief your staff.** This document explains how. Should take 5 minutes per staff member.
3. **Tell us what's broken.** WhatsApp the founder directly: +27 \***\*\_\*\***. Don't be polite. Ugly feedback is faster than nice feedback.

## What we're NOT asking you to do

- We do not collect customer phone numbers. We removed SMS auth deliberately because it was unreliable. Customers sign up with email or Google — same as Netflix or Spotify.
- We do not text your customers. Period. They pull the app open when they want to.
- We do not take a cut of your sales. The R299 is the whole price.

## How customers actually use it

The first interaction is the only interaction that matters. If your staff doesn't pitch the app at the till, nothing else works. Here's the script we want every staff member to use:

> **"Are you on Area Code? Get a free [your reward] after 5 visits. Free to sign up."**

That's the whole script. 13 words.

If they say yes and they already have the app, ask them to tap the check-in button on their phone. Two seconds.

If they say yes but they don't have the app, give them the URL: **areacode.co.za**. They can sign up while they wait for their drink.

If they say no, drop it. Don't pitch twice.

## The First-Get token (the most important new thing)

Some customers will say yes but won't want to sign up at the till. That's fine. You issue them a one-time code from your staff phone. They take the code home, sign up at home, and the code gives them their first free reward already credited.

That means: **the customer leaves your venue having earned something for free, even if they never opened the app.**

This is the difference between Area Code and every other loyalty program. They cost a customer their time and their data before giving them anything. We give them the reward first.

To issue a token, the staff member opens the staff app on their phone, taps "Issue token" on the First-Get card, and reads the 8 characters out loud (or shows the screen, or prints the slip). The token is valid for 30 days.

## Why your staff should care

Two reasons.

**One:** the more redemptions a staff member processes, the higher they appear on the leaderboard. The owner sees the leaderboard. So do they. Top of the leaderboard at the end of the week is a real, visible thing.

**Two:** the leaderboard tracks something we call "attributed return visits" — customers who came back within 30 days of being served by a particular staff member. That's the ROI proof your staff member is creating, in numbers, every day.

If you reward your top staff member at the end of the month based on this leaderboard, this app earns its keep.

## What you'll see in the dashboard

- **Live panel** — who's checking in right now. Useful at the till, for quiet observation.
- **Reward metrics** — claim rate, time-to-claim, redemption rate.
- **Staff leaderboard** — who's pitching, who's not.
- **Reports** — peak hours, crowd composition, music profile, repeat-visitor rate. Updated every Monday.

If any of those are confusing, WhatsApp us. We'll either explain or fix.

## What success looks like in two weeks

You should see at least 30 customer signups from your venue. If you're seeing fewer than 10, something's wrong with the staff pitch. Call us.

Of those 30, you should see 10+ second visits within 7 days. If you're seeing fewer than 5, the reward isn't strong enough. Make it cheaper to earn or more valuable.

Both numbers are visible in the dashboard.

## What success looks like in three months

Your loyalty members should be 30% of your daily check-ins. Their average ticket should be 15-20% higher than non-members'. The leaderboard should show 80% of redemptions coming from 2-3 staff members — those are the people you want to keep.

If those numbers don't materialise, we got it wrong somewhere. Tell us.
